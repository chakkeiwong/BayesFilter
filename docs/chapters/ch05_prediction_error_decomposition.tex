\chapter{Prediction-Error Decomposition}
\label{ch:bf-prediction-error-decomposition}

The linear Gaussian model is the first BayesFilter reference target because its
likelihood is exact and its derivative recursions can be audited against code
and automatic-differentiation tests.  This chapter records the value-only
likelihood spine; score and Hessian recursions are deferred to
Chapters~\ref{ch:bf-kalman-score}--\ref{ch:bf-kalman-hessian}.

\section{Linear Gaussian Specialization}
\label{sec:bf-lgssm-specialization}

Under the contract in Definition~\ref{def:bf-ssm-contract}, the linear Gaussian
specialization is
\begin{align}
  x_{t+1} &= c(\theta) + F(\theta)x_t + u_{t+1},
  & u_{t+1} &\sim \calN(0,Q(\theta)), \label{eq:bf-lgssm-state} \\
  y_t &= a(\theta) + H(\theta)x_t + \varepsilon_t,
  & \varepsilon_t &\sim \calN(0,R(\theta)). \label{eq:bf-lgssm-observation}
\end{align}
For the exact likelihood backend, $Q(\theta)\succeq 0$ and
$R(\theta)\succ 0$ are required at every accepted target evaluation.  The
initial moments are denoted $m_{0|0}$ and $P_{0|0}$.

\section{Kalman Recursion}
\label{sec:bf-kalman-recursion}

Let
\[
  m_{t|s}=\E[x_t\mid y_{1:s}], \qquad
  P_{t|s}=\Var(x_t\mid y_{1:s}).
\]
The prediction step is
\begin{align}
  m_{t|t-1} &= c + Fm_{t-1|t-1}, \label{eq:bf-kalman-pred-mean}\\
  P_{t|t-1} &= FP_{t-1|t-1}F^\top + Q. \label{eq:bf-kalman-pred-cov}
\end{align}
The innovation and innovation covariance are
\begin{align}
  v_t &= y_t-a-Hm_{t|t-1}, \label{eq:bf-innovation}\\
  S_t &= HP_{t|t-1}H^\top + R. \label{eq:bf-innovation-cov}
\end{align}
When $S_t\succ 0$, the Kalman gain and filtered moments are
\begin{align}
  K_t &= P_{t|t-1}H^\top S_t^{-1}, \label{eq:bf-kalman-gain}\\
  m_{t|t} &= m_{t|t-1}+K_tv_t, \label{eq:bf-kalman-update-mean}\\
  P_{t|t} &= (I-K_tH)P_{t|t-1}. \label{eq:bf-kalman-update-cov}
\end{align}
Production square-root implementations may update an algebraically equivalent
factor rather than materializing \eqref{eq:bf-kalman-update-cov}.

\section{Log Likelihood}
\label{sec:bf-ped-loglik}

The prediction-error decomposition gives
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:bf-ped-loglik}
  \ell(\theta)
  =
  -\frac{1}{2}\sum_{t=1}^T
  \left[
    \log\det S_t
    + v_t^\top S_t^{-1}v_t
    + n_{y,t}\log(2\pi)
  \right],
\end{equation}
where $n_{y,t}$ is the number of observed components at time $t$.  In a dense
panel, $n_{y,t}=n_y$.  In a masked panel, the same formula applies after
selecting the observed components and the corresponding rows and columns of the
observation objects.

Equation~\eqref{eq:bf-ped-loglik} is the exact likelihood for
\eqref{eq:bf-lgssm-state}--\eqref{eq:bf-lgssm-observation}.  It is also the
reference value against which approximate nonlinear filters and alternative
linear algebra backends should be tested whenever a controlled linear Gaussian
case is available.

\section{Initialization}
\label{sec:bf-kalman-initialization}

Initial moments are part of the model contract.  If $m_{0|0}$ and $P_{0|0}$ are
fixed by design, derivative initial conditions in later chapters are zero.  If
$P_{0|0}$ is the stationary covariance, then its derivatives are governed by
the derivative Lyapunov equations for the transition system.  BayesFilter must
record which initialization policy defines the target, because changing it
changes the likelihood.
